About This Tool

This interactive map identifies areas where high crime density coincides with inadequate street lighting across New York City. Each grid cell covers a standardized area; the two slider thresholds let you define what counts as “high crime” and “low lighting.”

Crime data is derived from NYPD publicly available incident reports, aggregated into grid cells. Percentiles indicate how a cell compares to all other cells across the five boroughs.

Lighting data is sourced from high-resolution nighttime satellite rasters (measured in nanowatts per centimeter squared per steradian), capturing ambient illumination at the street level.

How to use: Adjust the lighting and crime sliders to narrow or widen the overlap. Lower the lighting percentile to focus on darker areas; raise the crime percentile to focus on higher-crime zones. Use the presets for common views, or type a specific address in the search bar.
